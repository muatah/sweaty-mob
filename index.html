The Founder

Before Sweaty Mob Was a Brand, It Was One Man and a Wild Idea.

It started during COVID. An exercise bike. An Xbox controller. And a PE teacher named Mo who refused to stop moving.

Muata Gibson-Hunter — Mo Rider to most — is a PE teacher, cyclist, father, and youth mentor based in the DMV. When the world locked down, Mo did what felt natural: he clipped into his exercise bike, loaded up his favorite game, and started pedaling. Not as a workout plan. Not as a brand. Just because sitting still wasn't an option.

But something clicked. The harder he pedaled, the sharper he played. The sweat kept coming, the wins kept stacking. Friends started watching. Then they started riding too.

"Everyone kept telling gamers to put the controller down. I decided to make the controller the reason they got up."

That instinct became a system. Mo brought exergaming into his PE classroom and watched the students who "hated exercise" become the most engaged in the building. He built active gaming protocols that pair cardio equipment with the games people already love. He designed fitness gaming workouts that weave bodyweight movements into the natural rhythm of a gaming session — before, during, and after play.

The name says everything. "Sweaty" because real work makes you drip. And in gaming culture, "sweaty" means you're so elite that nobody can keep up. "Mob" because none of this works alone. We move together.

8,000+ Community members worldwide

Today Sweaty Mob is a full ecosystem — exergaming guides, educator certifications, a school-based PE program at Sojourner Truth Montessori in DC, daily challenges, and a growing library of resources built to prove one thing: gaming and fitness are not opposites. They are the ultimate combination.

Mo still answers every message personally. That's not a tagline. It's a policy.
