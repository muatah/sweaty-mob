Our Mission

From One PE Classroom to 8,000+ Members

The Origin Story

It started in a PE classroom. Mo Rider (teacher, cyclist, gamer) noticed something that bothered him: kids who loved gaming but hated exercise. Adults who spent hours in front of screens but couldn't find 15 minutes to move. The problem wasn't motivation. It was method.

What if the games people already loved could become the workouts they needed? That question became Sweaty Mob. The name is the mission: "sweaty" because real workouts make you sweat, "mob" because nobody transforms alone.

Today, Sweaty Mob is a full LLC with 10 published ebooks, 5 certification tiers, a school PE program, and a community of 8,000+ members who prove every day that gaming and fitness aren't opposites. They're the ultimate power-up.

The Science Behind the System

The Sweaty Mob system isn't a gimmick. Exergaming, active gaming, and fitness gaming are each backed by real research. Studies show that combining physical activity with gaming produces measurably better outcomes than either alone:

  • 222% more energy expenditure compared to sedentary gaming sessions
  • 64% higher heart rates during active gaming vs. passive gaming
  • 35% better knowledge retention when physical activity is integrated with learning
  • Increased dopamine response from combined gaming + exercise stimuli
  • Improved social bonding through collaborative gaming workouts

Every Sweaty Mob product is built on this research foundation. A six-week HIIT program improved FPS reaction time by 8.1% and shooting accuracy by 4%. Exercise triggers BDNF release, priming your brain for faster processing. We don't ask people to stop gaming. We show them how to game better.

The School Program

Sojourner Truth Montessori School

Located in Washington DC, Sojourner Truth Montessori is the proving ground for Sweaty Mob Kids. Our PE curriculum integrates exergaming, active gaming, and fitness gaming into daily physical education, creating a model that other schools can adopt.

Program Impact

  • Students who previously refused to participate in PE are now actively engaged
  • Technology integration that meets students where they are
  • Collaborative gaming builds teamwork and social skills
  • Measurable improvements in fitness test scores
  • Student-led gaming workout clubs formed organically
  • Parent engagement increased through take-home gaming workout guides
